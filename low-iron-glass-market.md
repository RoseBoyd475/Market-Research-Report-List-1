<p><p>The low-iron glass market comprises various types, including rolled glass and float glass. Rolled glass is produced by passing molten glass through rollers, resulting in a textured surface ideal for applications like interiors and decorative elements. Float glass, on the other hand, is made by floating molten glass on top of molten tin, creating a smooth, even surface. Both types are valued for their enhanced transparency and reduced greenish tint, making them suitable for high-end architectural and solar panel applications.</p></p>
