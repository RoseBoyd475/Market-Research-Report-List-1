<p><ul><li>PFSA</li><li>PFA</li><li>FEP</li><li>ETFE</li><li>PVDF</li><li>PCTFE</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fluoropolymer membrane market comprises several types, each with unique properties and applications. PFSA (Perfluorosulfonic Acid) membranes are widely used in fuel cells. PFA (Perfluoroalkoxy) membranes offer high thermal stability and chemical resistance. FEP (Fluorinated Ethylene Propylene) membranes are known for their electrical insulation and low friction. ETFE (Ethylenetetrafluoroethylene) membranes are lightweight and durable, often used in architectural applications. PVDF (Polyvinylidene Fluoride) offers strength and chemical resistance, while PCTFE (Polychlorotrifluoroethylene) is known for its low permeability. Other types encompass various specialized membranes tailored for specific industrial needs.</p></p>
