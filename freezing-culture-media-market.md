<p><p>The freezing culture media market can be categorized into two main types: DMSO-based and DMSO-free options. DMSO (dimethyl sulfoxide) is commonly used as a cryoprotectant to prevent ice crystal formation during freezing, enhancing cell viability upon thawing. However, DMSO-free media are gaining traction due to concerns over toxicity and biological effects, offering safer alternatives for cell preservation. Both types cater to diverse research and therapeutic needs, with users selecting based on compatibility with specific cell types or applications.</p></p>
