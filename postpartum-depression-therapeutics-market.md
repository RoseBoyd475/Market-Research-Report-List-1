<p><p>The postpartum depression therapeutics market includes various types of antidepressants. Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) are commonly prescribed for their effectiveness and favorable side effect profile. Serotonin norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors (SNRIs) also target mood regulation, potentially enhancing treatment outcomes. Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs) are older medications, effective but with more side effects. Monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs) are less commonly used due to dietary restrictions and interactions, but they can be effective for certain patients. Each type plays a crucial role in managing postpartum depression.</p></p>
