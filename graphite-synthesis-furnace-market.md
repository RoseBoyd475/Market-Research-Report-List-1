<p><p>The Graphite Synthesis Furnace market comprises three primary types based on the synthesis gas used. Hydrogen Nitride Synthesis utilizes a combination of hydrogen and nitrogen gases to produce high-quality graphite by enhancing purity and structure. Hydrogen Synthesis focuses solely on hydrogen gas to achieve optimal material characteristics, typically resulting in better conductivity. Hydrogen Chloride Synthesis, on the other hand, employs hydrogen chloride to facilitate the conversion process, often improving the efficiency of graphite production by utilizing the unique chemical properties of chlorine.</p></p>
