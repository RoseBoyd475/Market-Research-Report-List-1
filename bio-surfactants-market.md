<p><p>The bio-surfactants market is categorized into three main types: anionic, cationic, and amphoteric surfactants. Anionic surfactants carry a negative charge and are effective in removing dirt and oil, commonly used in detergents. Cationic surfactants, with a positive charge, often exhibit antimicrobial properties and are used in fabric softeners and disinfectants. Amphoteric surfactants can act as either anionic or cationic depending on the pH, making them versatile for personal care products and industrial applications.</p></p>
