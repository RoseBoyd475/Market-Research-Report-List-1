<p><p>The melt-spun fiber market is primarily divided into two types: filament melt-spun fibers and staple melt-spun fibers. Filament melt-spun fibers consist of continuous long strands typically used in applications like textiles and industrial products, offering enhanced strength and durability. In contrast, staple melt-spun fibers are produced in shorter lengths, ideal for spinning into yarns. This distinction allows for varied applications, with filament fibers often utilized in high-performance textiles, while staple fibers are commonly used in apparel and home textiles.</p></p>
