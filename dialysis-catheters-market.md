<p><p>The dialysis catheters market is divided into long-term and short-term hemodialysis catheters. Long-term hemodialysis catheters are designed for extended use, typically lasting weeks to months, facilitating patients requiring chronic dialysis. In contrast, short-term hemodialysis catheters are intended for temporary use, often suited for acute situations, and generally remain in place for days to a week. Both types are essential for effective blood access in hemodialysis treatments, catering to varying patient needs based on their condition and treatment duration.</p></p>
